# Audience

## End Users
- University students
- Fresh graduates
- People actively looking to upskill

## Key Characteristics
- Time-constrained — short attention spans for learning content
- Goal-oriented — want to complete something that feels worthwhile
- Mobile-first behavior — accustomed to short-form video (reels, TikTok format)
- Price-sensitive — freemium model must feel fair

## Biggest Pain Points (known)
- Purchasing a bundle but not completing it — drop-off after purchase is the core problem
- Navigation that doesn't guide them through the right order
- Unclear progress — not knowing where they left off or how far they've come

## What They Want to Do Quickly
- Pick up where they left off
- Find content relevant to their goals or interests
- Complete a short video without friction
- Understand what they're buying before spending credits

## What Confuses Them
- The difference between For You, Bundle, and Lesson Plan
- How many credits something costs and whether it's worth it
- How to navigate between parts and chapters within a bundle

## Tone for App Copy
- Warm, direct, encouraging
- Conversational — like a knowledgeable friend, not a corporate platform
- Never stiff, formal, or overly professional
- Accessible to non-native English speakers where possible

## Claude as Internal User
- Non-technical designer-product owner
- Makes all product decisions solo
- Needs Claude to flag big technical changes clearly
- Uploads Figma, written specs, and qualitative feedback as primary inputs
